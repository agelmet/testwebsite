Eugenia Panagiotides
01 The teacher

Trained at the Aristotle University School of Drama and the Stella Adler Studio in New York, Eugenia has spent a decade making rooms feel safe enough to be brave in.

As children, we say yes without hesitation — we imagine, pretend and play without worrying how we look or whether we're good enough. Somewhere along the way, most of us lose that freedom. Eugenia's whole practice is about getting it back.

Through acting and improvisation, she invites beginners and seasoned performers alike to take risks, make mistakes, and grow through doing rather than judging — building skill little by little through play and genuine connection.

"We are aware of what we are doing, but we are not judging."
02 The approach

Creativity flourishes when people feel safe.

①

A place to explore

This isn't about perfection. It's about exploration — a space where mistakes are celebrated as part of the process and beginners are genuinely welcome.

②

Growth through doing

You learn acting by acting. We take risks, try new things, fail, learn, and try again — developing awareness of our choices without harsh self-criticism.

③

A supportive community

Strangers become an ensemble. Here you'll find a group that feels like family — where play, expression and personal growth happen side by side.
